# Design Rules — McKinsey Reference Style

These rules define the EXACT visual style for all generated slides. Follow every rule precisely.

---

## 1. Color Palette (STRICT — No Deviations)

The palette is deliberately restrained. Do NOT use neon, bright, or saturated colors.

| Role | Hex Code | Description |
|------|----------|-------------|
| Background | `#002244` | **Dark navy** — the slide background is ALWAYS this dark navy |
| Title text | `#FFFFFF` | White |
| Body text | `#FFFFFF` | White (primary) or `#B0C4DE` light steel blue (secondary) |
| Accent / highlight | `#4A90D9` | Medium blue — used sparingly for emphasis |
| Table headers | `#003366` | Slightly lighter navy |
| Table row alt | `#002B4E` | Subtle row striping |
| Divider lines | `#4A90D9` | Thin accent blue lines |
| Chart fills | `#4A90D9`, `#6BAAEE`, `#B0C4DE` | Blue family only |

### BANNED Colors
- No neon green, no cyan, no seafoam, no mint, no teal, no orange
- No `028090`, no `00A896`, no `02C39A`, no bright saturated accents
- No white backgrounds — the background is ALWAYS dark navy `#002244`

---

## 2. Slide Title (Insight-Driven "So-What" Statement)

### Rules:
- The title is NOT a simple topic label (e.g., ~~"Energy Strategy Overview"~~)
- The title MUST be an **insight-driven statement** that communicates the key takeaway
- It should read like a conclusion or finding, not a category name
- Keep it to 1-2 lines maximum

### Examples of good titles:
- "Global hydrogen demand is expected to grow 5-7x by 2050, driven by industrial decarbonization"
- "Three strategic levers can reduce production costs by 40% within the next decade"
- "Policy support remains the critical enabler, with $280B committed across major economies"

### Examples of BAD titles (never do this):
- ~~"Hydrogen Market Overview"~~
- ~~"Energy Strategy"~~
- ~~"Key Findings"~~

---

## 3. Title Divider Line (REQUIRED)

- Place a **thin horizontal line** directly below the title text
- Color: `#4A90D9` (accent blue)
- Width: spans most of the slide width (e.g., from left margin to right margin)
- Thickness: 1-1.5pt — thin and elegant, not bold
- Spacing: ~0.15" below the title text, ~0.2" above the body content
- This divider is a **signature element** — never omit it

---

## 4. Layout & Spacing

### Margins and Zones:
- **Left/Right margins**: 0.5"
- **Top margin**: 0.4" (for title)
- **Title zone**: top 0.4" to ~1.2" (title text + divider line)
- **Content zone**: starts at ~1.4" from top — NEVER overlaps the title zone
- **Footer zone**: bottom 0.3"

### CRITICAL: No Content-Title Overlap
- Content boxes, tables, charts MUST start BELOW the title + divider line
- Minimum gap between divider line and first content element: 0.2"
- The title must ALWAYS be fully visible and unobstructed

### Content Arrangement:
- Prefer **structured data tables** or **column-based layouts** for data
- Avoid unnecessary visual clutter (no decorative sidebars, no vertical text labels)
- Clean whitespace between elements
- No element labeled "ENERGY STRATEGY" or similar running vertically on the side

---

## 5. Data Display: Structured Tables

When presenting data, prefer clean structured tables:

- Table header row: background `#003366`, white bold text
- Table body rows: alternating `#002244` / `#002B4E` for subtle striping
- Cell text: white `#FFFFFF`, 12-14pt Calibri
- Cell padding: comfortable (not cramped)
- Borders: minimal — use thin `#4A90D9` lines or no visible borders (rely on row color alternation)
- Align numbers to the right, text to the left

If a timeline is needed, present it as a table with year columns, not as a graphical process flow.

---

## 6. Typography

| Element | Font | Size | Weight | Color |
|---------|------|------|--------|-------|
| Slide title | Arial or Calibri | 22-28pt | Bold | `#FFFFFF` |
| Subtitle / section label | Calibri | 16-18pt | Semibold | `#B0C4DE` |
| Body text | Calibri | 12-14pt | Regular | `#FFFFFF` |
| Table header | Calibri | 12-14pt | Bold | `#FFFFFF` |
| Table cell | Calibri | 11-13pt | Regular | `#FFFFFF` |
| Footer | Calibri | 9-10pt | Regular | `#8899AA` muted |
| Source notes | Calibri | 8-9pt | Italic | `#8899AA` muted |

Note: Do NOT use Georgia. Use Arial or Calibri exclusively.

---

## 7. Footer Format (EXACT)

- Position: bottom-right of the slide
- Text: `McKinsey & Company` followed by a page number
- Format: **McKinsey & Company    |    [Page #]** or simply **McKinsey & Company  [Page #]**
- Font: Calibri, 9pt, color `#8899AA`
- No other footer elements — no extra source attributions in the footer bar
- Source citations go in a small text box just above the footer, left-aligned, italic, 8-9pt

---

## 8. Things to NEVER Do

1. ❌ Use a white or light background
2. ❌ Use neon/bright/saturated accent colors (green, cyan, orange, teal)
3. ❌ Use a simple topic label as the slide title
4. ❌ Omit the thin horizontal divider line below the title
5. ❌ Allow any content element to overlap or obscure the title
6. ❌ Add vertical sidebar text (e.g., "ENERGY STRATEGY" running vertically)
7. ❌ Use complex graphical timelines when a clean table would suffice
8. ❌ Use Georgia font
9. ❌ Put source attributions in the footer bar itself
10. ❌ Use more than 2-3 colors beyond navy/white/light blue

---

## 9. Overall Aesthetic

The goal is **McKinsey-style professional consulting slides**:
- Restrained, sophisticated color palette (navy + blues + white only)
- Clean layouts with generous whitespace
- Data-forward: structured tables, clear numbers
- Insight-driven titles that tell the story
- Minimal decoration — every element earns its place
- Dark navy background conveys authority and premium quality

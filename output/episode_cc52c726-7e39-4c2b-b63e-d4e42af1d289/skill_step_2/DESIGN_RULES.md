# Design Rules — McKinsey Reference Style

These rules define the EXACT visual style for all generated slides. Follow every rule precisely.

---

## 1. Color Palette (STRICT — No Deviations)

The palette is deliberately restrained. Do NOT use neon, bright, or saturated colors.

| Role | Hex Code | Description |
|------|----------|-------------|
| Background | `#002244` | **Dark navy** — the slide background is ALWAYS this dark navy. NEVER white. |
| Title text | `#FFFFFF` | White |
| Body text | `#FFFFFF` | White (primary) or `#B0C4DE` light steel blue (secondary) |
| Accent / highlight | `#4A90D9` | Medium blue — used sparingly for emphasis |
| Divider lines | `#4A90D9` | Thin accent blue lines |
| Chart fills | `#4A90D9`, `#6BAAEE`, `#B0C4DE` | Blue family only |

### BANNED Colors
- No neon green, no cyan, no seafoam, no mint, no teal, no orange
- No `028090`, no `00A896`, no `02C39A`, no bright saturated accents
- **No white backgrounds — the background is ALWAYS dark navy `#002244`**
- **No `#FFFFFF` or any light color as the slide background — EVER**

---

## 2. Slide Title (Insight-Driven "So-What" Statement)

### Rules:
- The title is NOT a simple topic label (e.g., ~~"Energy Strategy Overview"~~)
- The title MUST be an **insight-driven statement** that communicates the key takeaway
- It should read like a conclusion or finding, not a category name
- Keep it to 1-2 lines maximum

### Title Typography — SIGNATURE SERIF FONT (CRITICAL):
- **Font: Georgia** (serif font) — this is the signature style element
- Georgia gives the title an authoritative, editorial quality that distinguishes McKinsey slides
- Size: 22-26pt
- Weight: **Bold**
- Color: `#FFFFFF` (white)
- **DO NOT use Arial or Calibri for the title** — the serif font is a defining characteristic

### Examples of good titles:
- "Global hydrogen demand is expected to grow 5-7x by 2050, driven by industrial decarbonization"
- "Three strategic levers can reduce production costs by 40% within the next decade"
- "Policy support remains the critical enabler, with $280B committed across major economies"

### Examples of BAD titles (never do this):
- ~~"Hydrogen Market Overview"~~
- ~~"Energy Strategy"~~
- ~~"Key Findings"~~

---

## 3. Title Divider Line (REQUIRED)

- Place a **thin horizontal line** directly below the title text
- Color: `#4A90D9` (accent blue)
- Width: spans most of the slide width (e.g., from left margin to right margin)
- Thickness: 1-1.5pt — thin and elegant, not bold
- Spacing: ~0.15" below the title text, ~0.2" above the body content
- This divider is a **signature element** — never omit it

---

## 4. Layout & Spacing

### Margins and Zones:
- **Left/Right margins**: 0.5"
- **Top margin**: 0.4" (for title)
- **Title zone**: top 0.4" to ~1.2" (title text + divider line)
- **Content zone**: starts at ~1.4" from top — NEVER overlaps the title zone
- **Bottom content limit**: content must END by 6.4" from top (leave generous bottom margin)
- **Source note zone**: 6.5" – 6.7" from top
- **Footer zone**: bottom 0.3" (7.0" – 7.5")

### CRITICAL: No Overlapping Elements
- Content boxes, tables, charts MUST start BELOW the title + divider line
- Minimum gap between divider line and first content element: 0.2"
- The title must ALWAYS be fully visible and unobstructed
- **Source text must NEVER overlap with table rows or content elements**
- **Leave at least 0.3" gap between the bottom of any table/content and the source note**

### Content Arrangement:
- Use **clean, open tables** or **column-based layouts** for data
- Avoid unnecessary visual clutter (no decorative sidebars, no vertical text labels)
- **Generous whitespace** between and around elements — do not cram content
- No element labeled "ENERGY STRATEGY" or similar running vertically on the side

---

## 5. Data Display: MINIMALIST Open Tables

**CRITICAL: Tables must be clean, open, and minimalist — NOT dense or heavy.**

### Table Design Philosophy:
The reference style uses an **open, airy table design** — think more like a well-typeset financial report than an Excel spreadsheet. The table should feel like it "breathes."

### Table Style Rules:
- **NO full grid lines** — do NOT draw borders around every cell
- **NO colored cell backgrounds** — do NOT fill cells with `#003366`, `#002B4E`, or any color
- **Table cells have a TRANSPARENT / no-fill background** — the dark navy slide background shows through
- Use **only thin horizontal rules** to separate sections:
  - One thin line below the header row (`#4A90D9`, 0.75pt)
  - Optionally one thin line above a totals/summary row
  - NO vertical lines between columns
  - NO horizontal lines between every row
- **Header row**: white bold text, NO background fill — just separated by the thin line beneath
- **Body rows**: white regular text, no background fill, no row striping
- Cell text: white `#FFFFFF`, 12-14pt Calibri
- **Generous row height** — at least 0.4" per row so content doesn't feel cramped
- **Generous cell padding** — at least 0.15" internal padding
- Align numbers to the right, text to the left
- **Table must not extend below 6.4" from top** to prevent overlap with source/footer

### What NOT to do with tables:
- ❌ Full grid borders around every cell
- ❌ Colored/filled cell backgrounds (no `#003366`, no `#002B4E` fills)
- ❌ Heavy or thick border lines
- ❌ Dense, cramped rows with small padding
- ❌ Row striping with alternating background colors
- ❌ Table extending into the source/footer zone

If a timeline is needed, present it as a clean table with year columns, not as a graphical process flow.

---

## 6. Typography

| Element | Font | Size | Weight | Color |
|---------|------|------|--------|-------|
| **Slide title** | **Georgia (serif)** | **22-26pt** | **Bold** | `#FFFFFF` |
| Subtitle / section label | Calibri | 16-18pt | Semibold | `#B0C4DE` |
| Body text | Calibri | 12-14pt | Regular | `#FFFFFF` |
| Table header | Calibri | 12-14pt | Bold | `#FFFFFF` |
| Table cell | Calibri | 11-13pt | Regular | `#FFFFFF` |
| Footer | Calibri | 9-10pt | Regular | `#8899AA` muted |
| Source notes | Calibri | 8-9pt | Italic | `#8899AA` muted |

### IMPORTANT — Title Font Rule:
- The slide title MUST use **Georgia** (a serif font). This is the signature typographic choice.
- All OTHER text (body, tables, footer, source) uses **Calibri** (sans-serif).
- The contrast between serif title and sans-serif body is a key design characteristic.
- **Do NOT use Arial or Calibri for the slide title.**

---

## 7. Footer Format (EXACT)

- Position: bottom-right of the slide
- Text: `McKinsey & Company` followed by a page number
- Format: **McKinsey & Company    |    [Page #]** or simply **McKinsey & Company  [Page #]**
- Font: Calibri, 9pt, color `#8899AA`
- No other footer elements — no extra source attributions in the footer bar
- Source citations go in a small text box ABOVE the footer, left-aligned, italic, 8-9pt
- **Source text must be positioned at 6.5"–6.7" from top — well below all content**

---

## 8. Things to NEVER Do

1. ❌ Use a white or light background — **background is ALWAYS `#002244` dark navy**
2. ❌ Use neon/bright/saturated accent colors (green, cyan, orange, teal)
3. ❌ Use a simple topic label as the slide title
4. ❌ Omit the thin horizontal divider line below the title
5. ❌ Allow any content element to overlap or obscure the title
6. ❌ Allow source text to overlap with table rows or content
7. ❌ Add vertical sidebar text (e.g., "ENERGY STRATEGY" running vertically)
8. ❌ Use complex graphical timelines when a clean table would suffice
9. ❌ Use Arial or Calibri for the slide title (use **Georgia serif** instead)
10. ❌ Put source attributions in the footer bar itself
11. ❌ Use more than 2-3 colors beyond navy/white/light blue
12. ❌ Use full grid lines or colored cell backgrounds in tables
13. ❌ Create dense, cramped table layouts — always leave generous whitespace

---

## 9. Overall Aesthetic

The goal is **McKinsey-style professional consulting slides**:
- Restrained, sophisticated color palette (navy + blues + white only)
- **Dark navy background (`#002244`) — ALWAYS, no exceptions**
- Clean layouts with **generous whitespace and breathing room**
- Data-forward: **minimalist open tables** with thin horizontal rules only
- **Serif title font (Georgia)** contrasting with sans-serif body (Calibri)
- Insight-driven titles that tell the story
- Minimal decoration — every element earns its place
- **No overlapping elements** — source text well separated from content
- Professional polish: nothing cramped, nothing overlapping, nothing cluttered

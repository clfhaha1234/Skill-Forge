# Footer Rules — McKinsey Standard Footer

## Footer is MANDATORY
Every slide MUST include a properly formatted footer. No exceptions.

## Footer Components

### 1. Footer Separator Line
- Thin horizontal rule spanning the full content width
- Position: Y=6.55", from X=0.75" to X=12.58"
- Color: `D6DCE4` (light gray)
- Weight: 0.5pt
- Purpose: Cleanly separates body content from footer metadata

### 2. Source Attribution (Left Side)
- Position: X=0.75", Y=6.62", W=9.0", H=0.35"
- Text format: "Source: [Primary source]; [Secondary source]; McKinsey analysis"
- Font: Arial or Calibri, **8pt**, italic, color `999999`
- Alignment: Left
- Always include at least one source. If the slide contains analysis or data, cite it.
- Examples:
  - "Source: IEA World Energy Outlook 2023; BloombergNEF; McKinsey Energy Insights"
  - "Source: Company annual reports; expert interviews; McKinsey analysis"
  - "Source: McKinsey Global Institute analysis"

### 3. McKinsey & Company Branding + Page Number (Right Side) — CRITICAL
- **This is MANDATORY. Every slide must include "McKinsey & Company" in the bottom-right footer area.**
- Position: X=10.5", Y=6.62", W=2.08", H=0.35"
- Text: **"McKinsey & Company"** followed by a separator and the page number
  - Format option A: `McKinsey & Company    1` (firm name then page number, right-aligned)
  - Format option B: Two separate text boxes — "McKinsey & Company" and then page number "1" to its right
- Font: Arial or Calibri, **8pt**, regular (not italic), color `999999`
- Alignment: Right
- The **"McKinsey & Company"** text MUST appear on every slide. This is a firm branding requirement.

### IMPORTANT: Do NOT omit "McKinsey & Company"
The footer right side is NOT just a page number. It MUST include the firm attribution 
"McKinsey & Company" alongside or just before the page number. This is a key branding 
element visible on all reference slides.

## Positioning Rules
- Footer elements MUST stay within Y=6.55" to Y=7.10"
- Body content MUST NOT extend below Y=6.50"
- There must be at least 0.05" gap between the lowest body content and the footer separator line
- Footer text must NEVER overlap with body content


## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Forgetting "McKinsey & Company" in the footer** — this is the #1 most common mistake
- Forgetting the footer entirely
- Placing the footer too high (overlapping with content)
- Using body text size (12pt+) for footer — it should be small (8–9pt)
- Omitting the page number
- Using dark/bold text for footer (it should be subtle and muted)
- Having only a page number on the right side without "McKinsey & Company"

# Title Rules — Insight-Driven "So-What" Statements

## Core Principle
Every slide title MUST be an **insight-driven statement** that communicates the key 
takeaway of the slide. The title alone should tell the reader what they need to know, 
even if they don't read anything else on the slide.

## What Makes a Good Title

### Formula: [Specific finding] + [quantification if possible] + [implication or driver]

### Examples of GOOD Titles (Insight-Driven)
- "Green hydrogen costs have declined 40% since 2015, primarily driven by electrolyzer scale-up"
- "Three regulatory barriers account for 70% of delayed hydrogen projects in Europe"
- "China leads global electrolyzer capacity with 60% market share, creating supply chain risk"
- "Total addressable market for clean hydrogen will reach $150B by 2030 under current policy trajectories"
- "Switching to hydrogen fuel cells reduces fleet operating costs by 25% over a 10-year horizon"

### Examples of BAD Titles (Topic Labels — NEVER Use These)
- "Hydrogen Market Overview" ✗
- "Key Trends" ✗
- "Industry Analysis" ✗
- "Cost Comparison" ✗
- "Market Size and Growth" ✗
- "Competitive Landscape" ✗
- "Summary of Findings" ✗
- "Next Steps" ✗

## CRITICAL: Title Font — MUST BE SERIF (Georgia)
- **Font**: **Georgia** (serif font) — this is mandatory and non-negotiable
- **Size**: 22–26pt
- **Weight**: **Bold**
- **Color**: `333333` (dark charcoal)
- **Alignment**: **Left-aligned**
- **Position**: X=0.75", Y=0.55"
- **Max width**: 11.83" (within margins)
- **Max lines**: 2 (if title wraps, allocate H=0.70" for the text box)
- If the title is too long to fit in 2 lines at 22pt, shorten the insight — don't shrink the font

### WHY GEORGIA (SERIF)?
The reference slides ALL use a **serif font** for slide titles. This creates a distinctive, 
authoritative look that contrasts with the sans-serif body text. The serif title is a 
signature element of the McKinsey consulting style:
- **Title** → Georgia (serif) — conveys authority and gravitas
- **Everything else** → Arial/Calibri (sans-serif) — clean and readable

**NEVER use Arial or Calibri for the title.** The serif/sans-serif contrast between 
title and body is a key visual signature.

## Title Divider Line (REQUIRED)
- Immediately below the title text box
- Horizontal line from X=0.75" to X=12.58"
- Y position: 0.10–0.15" below the bottom of the title text box
- Color: `003A70` (navy), weight 1.5pt
- This line is MANDATORY on every slide

## Subtitle (Optional)
- Only use if additional context is needed (e.g., time period, geography, methodology note)
- Font: Arial or Calibri (sans-serif), 13–15pt, regular (not bold)
- Color: `666666`
- Position: just below the title, above the divider line
- Example: "Based on analysis of 45 projects across 12 countries, 2020–2023"

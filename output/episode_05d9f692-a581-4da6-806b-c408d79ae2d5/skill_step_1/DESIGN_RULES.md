# McKinsey-Style Slide Design Rules

## CRITICAL PRINCIPLES
1. **White background** — ALWAYS use a clean white (`FFFFFF`) background. Never use dark backgrounds.
2. **Insight-driven title** — The title must be a "so-what" statement (an actionable insight or conclusion), NOT a generic topic label. Example: Instead of "Dutch Hydrogen Strategy", write "The Netherlands can capture €15B in value by accelerating green hydrogen scale-up across three priority corridors".
3. **Minimalist, restrained design** — Less is more. Avoid heavy graphics, thick borders, and solid-filled boxes. Use white space generously.
4. **Professional polish** — Every element must be precisely aligned, consistently spaced, and purposefully placed.

## Color Palette (MANDATORY — McKinsey Brand)
- **Primary text**: Dark navy `#003A70` or near-black `#1A1A2E`
- **Secondary text / labels**: Medium grey `#666666` or `#58595B`
- **Accent (sparingly)**: McKinsey blue `#003A70` or `#0072CE` — used only for key highlights
- **Backgrounds**: Pure white `#FFFFFF` only
- **Borders / lines**: Light grey `#CCCCCC` or `#D9D9D9`
- **DO NOT USE**: Teal, cyan, seafoam, mint, bright green, or any saturated/vibrant colors
- Keep the palette to 3 colors maximum: navy, grey, and white

## Typography
- **Title**: Georgia or similar serif font, 22–28pt, bold, dark navy `#003A70`
- **Subtitle / section headers**: Calibri or Arial, 14–16pt, bold, dark navy or dark grey
- **Body text**: Calibri or Arial, 11–14pt, regular, dark grey `#333333`
- **Source / footnote text**: Calibri or Arial, 8–9pt, regular, medium grey `#666666`
- **Numbers / data**: Calibri or Arial, can be bold for emphasis

## Mandatory Structural Elements

### Thin Divider Line Under Title
- A thin horizontal rule (0.75pt–1pt weight) MUST appear directly below the title text
- Color: light grey `#CCCCCC` or medium grey `#999999`
- Spans nearly the full width of the slide (from left margin to right margin)
- Separates the title/insight from the body content

### McKinsey Footer
- A proper footer MUST appear at the bottom of every slide
- Contains: confidentiality notice (e.g., "CONFIDENTIAL"), source attribution, and page number
- Footer text: 8pt, grey `#666666`, left-aligned (confidentiality) and right-aligned (page number)
- A thin grey line (`#CCCCCC`, 0.5–0.75pt) separates the footer from the body content

### Page Number
- Appears in the bottom-right corner of the footer area
- Format: just the number (e.g., "1") or "Page 1"
- 8–9pt, grey `#666666`

### Source Attribution
- Left-aligned in the footer area, or just above the footer line
- Format: "Source: [description]" in 8–9pt grey text
- Must have good contrast against white background (grey on white, NOT white on dark)

## Layout Rules
- **Margins**: 0.5" on all sides minimum
- **Content spacing**: 0.3–0.5" between major content blocks
- **Alignment**: All elements must snap to a consistent grid; left-align text by default
- **Tables**: Use structured data tables with thin grey gridlines (`#D9D9D9`), no heavy borders
- **Boxes**: If boxes are needed, use thin borders (`0.75pt`) in light grey with NO fill, or very light grey fill (`#F5F5F5`). NEVER use solid colored fills.
- **Lines**: Always thin (0.5–1pt). Never thick or heavy.

## Content Structure (Top to Bottom)
1. **Title area** (top): Insight-driven title in serif font, bold, navy
2. **Divider line**: Thin grey horizontal rule
3. **Body content** (middle): Charts, tables, diagrams, or text — clean and minimal
4. **Source line**: "Source: ..." text in small grey
5. **Footer line**: Thin grey horizontal rule
6. **Footer area** (bottom): Confidentiality + page number

## What to AVOID
- ❌ Dark or colored backgrounds
- ❌ Solid-filled boxes or shapes with saturated colors
- ❌ Thick borders or lines (anything > 1.5pt)
- ❌ Generic topic-label titles (e.g., "Market Overview", "Strategy Update")
- ❌ Centered source text — always left-align
- ❌ Bright or vibrant accent colors (teal, cyan, green, orange)
- ❌ Clunky, heavy visual elements — keep everything light and refined
- ❌ Missing footer, missing page number, missing divider line

# Title Writing Rules — Insight-Driven Titles

## The #1 Rule
Every slide title MUST be an insight-driven "so-what" statement, NOT a topic label.

## What is an insight-driven title?
It's a sentence (or phrase) that communicates the KEY TAKEAWAY of the slide. If a busy executive reads only the title, they should understand the main point.

## Formula
**[Subject] + [action/verb] + [quantified outcome or specific insight]**

## Examples by Topic

| Topic | ❌ Wrong (Topic Label) | ✅ Correct (Insight Statement) |
|-------|----------------------|-------------------------------|
| Hydrogen | "Dutch Hydrogen Strategy" | "Three priority corridors could position the Netherlands as Europe's leading hydrogen hub by 2030" |
| Market | "Market Overview" | "Market share erosion accelerated to 3.2pp in Q3, driven by pricing pressure in the mid-segment" |
| Costs | "Cost Analysis" | "Consolidating procurement across 4 categories would yield €45M in annual savings" |
| Digital | "Digital Transformation" | "Digitizing the top 5 customer journeys could improve NPS by 15 points and reduce cost-to-serve by 20%" |
| Operations | "Supply Chain Performance" | "Lead times have increased 40% since 2021, creating a €12M working capital drag" |

## Title Formatting
- Font: Georgia (serif), 22–28pt, bold
- Color: Dark navy `#003A70`
- Position: Top-left of slide, left-aligned
- Length: 1–2 lines maximum (aim for one clear sentence)
- ALWAYS followed by a thin grey divider line below

# Title Writing Rules — Insight-Driven Titles

## The #1 Rule
Every slide title MUST be an insight-driven "so-what" statement, NOT a topic label.

## What is an insight-driven title?
It's a sentence (or phrase) that communicates the KEY TAKEAWAY of the slide. If a busy executive reads only the title, they should understand the main point.

## Formula
**[Subject] + [action/verb] + [quantified outcome or specific insight]**

## Examples by Topic

| Topic | ❌ Wrong (Topic Label) | ✅ Correct (Insight Statement) |
|-------|----------------------|-------------------------------|
| Hydrogen | "Dutch Hydrogen Strategy" | "Three priority corridors could position NL as Europe's hydrogen hub by 2030" |
| Market | "Market Overview" | "Market share erosion accelerated to 3.2pp in Q3, driven by mid-segment pricing" |
| Costs | "Cost Analysis" | "Consolidating procurement across 4 categories would yield €45M annual savings" |
| Digital | "Digital Transformation" | "Digitizing top 5 customer journeys could improve NPS by 15pts, cut costs 20%" |
| Operations | "Supply Chain Performance" | "Lead times up 40% since 2021, creating €12M working capital drag" |

## Title Formatting — PRECISE SIZING RULES
- Font: Georgia (serif), **18–22pt** (NOT larger — larger sizes cause wrapping/overlap)
- Color: Dark navy `#003A70`
- Position: Top-left of slide, left-aligned, starting at x=0.5", y=0.4"
- **Text box width: 8.5"–9.0"** (use nearly full slide width to prevent awkward wrapping)
- **Text box height: 0.7" max** (constrains to 1–2 lines)
- Length: **Maximum 100–120 characters** (aim for one clear sentence that fits in 1–2 lines at 20pt)
- ALWAYS followed by a thin grey divider line below, at y=1.2"

## CRITICAL: Title Must Not Overlap Divider Line
- The title text must END above y=1.1" on the slide
- The divider line sits at y=1.2"
- There must be a visible 0.1" gap between the bottom of the title text and the divider line
- If the title is too long and would extend below y=1.1", SHORTEN THE TITLE TEXT — do not increase font size or let it overflow
- Prefer concise, punchy titles over long, detailed ones

## Shortening Strategies
If an insight title is too long:
1. Remove "The Netherlands" → use "NL" or "Dutch"
2. Remove filler words ("that", "which", "in order to")
3. Combine clauses ("X and Y" instead of "X, which leads to Y")
4. Round numbers ("~€15B" instead of "€14.7 billion")
5. Use symbols: "%" not "percent", "€" not "euros", "→" for "leading to"
